Droplings for Mac

Quickly share files through your Dropbox, using custom themes.

I really like those menubar icon sharing apps. Drag a file from the Finder to a menubar icon, it'll be uploaded to the moon the cloud, and the link to its fancy new preview page is copied to the clipboard.

So useful, but every app I came across raised the same two questions for me:

  1. I already have a Dropbox account, why use yet another service?
  2. Why do my generated preview pages have to look like everyone else's?

So I wrote Droplings.

Download Droplings 0.9.4

Droplings is free at the moment as the 0.9.x versions are considered functional betas. However, when Droplings reaches 1.0, it'll be a paid app. (Not to worry, tho, its price will be less $10.)

Dropbox-Powered

You already have a Dropbox account. If it's a paid one, you have 100GB+ at your disposal. So why not use that one for your simple sharing needs? With it's public-facing ~/Dropbox/Public folder it makes it easy to put static files online. Droplings utilizes that feature.

Custom Themes

I'm a freelancer. Often times I need to send files to my clients. Many sharing services out there build nice preview pages around my file, yet all those pages look alike. I'd like to go the extra mile here.

Thus, you can either go with Droplings' default theme, or you create your own Mustache-based theme — check out the documentation.

No Size Limits

None of those silly "25MB max" limits! The sky's the limit, and by "sky" I mean your Dropbox account. According to Dropbox' FAQs the maximum file size is only restricted by your account.

And if you'll pass it a folder, it'll zip it up before uploading.

Here's Your Link

The second you drop a file on the menubar icon, the preview page link is copied to your clipboard, even if the file is still uploading. And by the time you're done writing that email, your file usually is online, too.

Requirements

  • a free or paid Dropbox account
  • OS X 10.6 Snow Leopard or higher
  • a Mac with 64bit architecture (i.e. pretty much any Mac since 2006)
